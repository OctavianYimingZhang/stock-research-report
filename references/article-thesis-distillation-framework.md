# Article Thesis Distillation Framework

Use outside articles, public analyst notes, and user-supplied thesis notes to
discover the market's thesis path, not to prove facts.

## Required Map

For each outside thesis path, capture:

- source title or locator
- source type and date
- thesis summary
- implied causal chain
- claimed catalyst
- claimed bottleneck
- claimed order or customer proof
- evidence used by the source
- evidence missing
- unsupported claims
- falsification test
- usable angle after verification

## Verification Rule

Run two tracks:

1. Outside-in: extract what the article believes, what proof it uses, and what
   evidence is missing.
2. Inside-out: rebuild the thesis from filings, investor relations, transcripts,
   regulator/government records, customer/partner disclosures, and market data.

Only overlapping or explicitly resolved claims can support valuation, short-risk
assessment, or the trade plan. Unsupported article claims become data gaps or
scenario assumptions.

## Output Integration

The final report should briefly show what outside thesis survived verification.
Do not quote or copy the article's wording. State confirmed, rejected, and
unresolved parts in the relevant narrative section.
