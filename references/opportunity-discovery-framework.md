# Opportunity Discovery Framework

Classify the stock before deciding section emphasis. The report should not use
the same template weight for every issuer.

## Archetypes

- `scarcity_bottleneck`
- `policy_protected_supply_chain`
- `customer_funded_capacity_ramp`
- `operating_leverage_recovery`
- `order_backlog_repricing`
- `strategic_asset_optional_value`
- `levered_residual_equity`
- `early_commercialization`
- `mature_infrastructure_compounder`
- `commodity_or_resource_processing`

If no company-specific route is supported, classify the name as industry beta
or watchlist-only and cap the trade conclusion.

## Four-Part Opportunity Test

High-conviction reports must answer:

1. Demand expansion: who is spending, why now, and whether demand is structural
   or cyclical.
2. Scaling difficulty: why supply cannot expand quickly; include qualification,
   certification, capex, construction time, yield, customer approval, regulation,
   financing, or channel barriers.
3. Bottleneck or scarcity: the exact value-chain node, why this company controls
   or benefits from it, and whether scarcity is temporary, cyclical, or
   structural.
4. Commercialization visibility: separate recognized revenue, binding purchase
   order, firm backlog, paid reservation, customer-funded capacity, contract,
   framework/IDIQ, pilot, MOU, LOI, pipeline, and management aspiration.

## Report Effect

The archetype changes the emphasis:

- scarcity and policy routes need industry-chain and bottleneck depth
- capacity-ramp routes need asset, funding, utilization, and execution depth
- order routes need order-quality and order-to-revenue depth
- levered routes need debt, maturity, dilution, and residual-equity depth
- mature compounders need reinvestment, cash conversion, and capital allocation
  depth
